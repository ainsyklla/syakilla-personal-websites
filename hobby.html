°‧★Bukit H, Kedah°‧★

The Bukit H hill in Kedah was the first hill I ever climbed. This trail posed quite a challenge with its steep incline and rugged terrain filled with small rocks. It was an unforgettable experience as I undertook this climb with my classmate from university.

°‧★Gua Bama,Pahang°‧★

This limestone hill, known as the Bama Cave, marks the first cave I ascended. Positioned alongside the road from Kuala Lipis to Musang Cave near the town of Padang Tengku, the Bama Cave stretches approximately 60 meters in height and 400 meters in length. Upon reaching the summit of this limestone formation, a panoramic vista of the encompassing locales, including Kampung Relong, Bapong, Berchang, and Berang, unfolds before the observer.

°‧★Gua Musang,Kelantan°‧★

The photograph captures my associates from the STPM program engaged in an ascent of the limestone caverns near the Gua Musang Railway Station in Kelantan. The limestone expanse, standing at an elevation of 1,200 meters, presents a formidable challenge. Our team invested more than an hour in the successful completion of this climb. The panoramic view from the summit encompassed the meticulously arranged township of Gua Musang, along with the main thoroughfare circuit.

°‧★Bukit Seladang,Pahang°‧★

Bukit Seladang, also known as Bukit Pohon Sebatang, is located in the Jerantut district in the state of Pahang Darul Makmur, just 3 kilometers from Jerantut Town. The climb has three designated checkpoints: CP 1 (294m), CP 2 (370m), and CP 3 (444m). Many climbers stop at CP 1 and CP 2 for the views.

°‧★Bukit Durian,Kelantan°‧★

The images depict my visit to Bukit Durian, where I engaged in weekend activities with my friends. The trail proved to be moderately challenging, offering a relaxed walking experience with an elevation of 260 meters. The area's allure lies in its scenic beauty, featuring a picturesque lake and providing opportunities for cycling and jogging.
